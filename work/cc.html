UX/UI

Crowd Capital

Operating at the intersection of real estate, investment platforms, and crowd funding.

Overview

Telling the story of an emerging startup through it's website.

The Project

Crowd Capital (CC) is a startup that combines social impact and crowdfunded investments.

The Problem

How to communicate a complex platform to users who aren't familiar with this space.

The Outcome

Spec'd UI wireframes handed off to offshore development team.

My Role

UX/UI Designer

My Team

The Founder, a Project Manager, a Visual Designer, and a Lead Engineer.

My Contribution

Lead testing, low/high wireframes, prototype, and supported the visual brand.

Summary

The problem was that Crowd Capital had not developed a brand identity yet.

As they worked with a Visual Designer to flesh out their visual brand, I was brought on board to redesign their website using the new brand guide.

The redesign’s goal was to develop a user journey that articulated Crowd Capital’s mission by supporting the team as they wrote the copy, and the Visual Designer’s brand guide. The challenge was figuring out how to simplify complex finance ideas to new types of investors for this platform.

On-Boarding Exploration

When I was brought on board, they already started developing the UI.

I had feedback and questions for their initial design rationale. In our discussion it seemed that they went straight to UI so I suggested we take a step back to look at the overall information architecture.

Afterwards, we focused on content. While there were opportunities to reduce or add content, most of it was predetermined beforehand. Throughout this project I tracked any changes we made in these site maps.

For the user journey I wanted to present the mission like an essay. The home page is the introduction, followed by the body paragraph where you can read more in-depth, and a conclusion or call to action to register.

Ideation

Next was to figure out how content would be organized.

I made quick sketches of how content would be organized. To speed up our timeline, I decided to design for mobile first and do desktop for the final UI. I felt that designing for mobile first helped prioritize our most to least important sections.

As we progressed, the copy and wireframes iterated concurrently. While I did play with some interactive assets like menus and modals, the wireframes primarily served as a framework for our copy.

First-Click Testing

User testing focused on registration and user journey.

The purpose of this test was to see how users would start their journey and to verify if they knew how to register for an account. In all 3 tasks we had an 80% success rate with 10 participants.

User Interface

UI focused on capturing attention.

My team tried their best to reduce the amount of copy we had, but in the end I had to work with what I had. To avoid information overload and enhance scan-ability, I used large and heavy heading type for contrast.

Reflection

Looking back, I wished I spent more time testing our ideas.

At the time I didn’t fully comprehend “why” I should be testing other than doing it because that’s what I’m supposed to do. I wish I interviewed participants and gathered more qualitative data. It would have given us a better idea if we were successful at articulating our message which was the whole point of this project.
