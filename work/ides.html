Research/UX

Illinois Department of Employment Security

A school project where we learned you can't redesign an entire site [well] in 10 weeks or less.

Overview

Improving the unemployment insurance application

The Project

IDES provides unemployment services for Illinois residents. We did not collaborate with IDES.

The Problem

Completing the UI application led to many questions and a poor UX.

Role & Team

As part of a 3-person design team, we collaborated on the entire design process.

Platform

Intended to be a redesign on their live site.

Deliverables

Research, wireframes, prototype.

Timeline

April to June 2020

Summary

Improving the unemployment application process during COVID.

Due to the recent developments in COVID-19, the effects of Illinois’ stay-at-home orders have laid off or furloughed many workers since March 21, 2020.This has led to an increase in activity for the Illinois Department of Employment Security’s (IDES) website, where many of those workers have gone to submit an unemployment insurance (UI) claim, and certify their benefits. The increase in use has provided more exposure to the prevalent issues that exist within the website and its user experience.

In this project, our team sought to rectify some of these issues by redesigning the IDES website for a mobile design. First, we conducted a competitive review of other states’ unemployment websites, TurboTax, and Amazon, to gain insight on how other sites have designed user input forms and information pages.

Afterwards, we conducted a heuristic evaluation to find opportunities for improvement in the current IDES site. Then we created a site map, and used it as a baseline to improve the information architecture through card sort and treejack tests. At the same time we facilitated a survey and live user interviews of people who were recently unemployed within the past three months, and completed a UI claims application online.

As a result of the tests, and the time constraints of completing a project within an academic quarter, we narrowed the scope of our project to redesigning the UI claims application only.
