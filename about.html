About Me

Hi, I'm Joseph

The intersection of all my experiences so far informs the way I collaborate and iterate.

Before I became a Designer, I helped facilitate social service and mental health programs for low-income families, foster youth, and people who wanted to die by suicide.

As a Case Manager, I learned that I was most effective when I took time to understand my client's needs. I found that oftentimes people kind of already know what the solutions are, my role was to help fill those gaps and probe further on what makes them frustrated.

As a Manager, I learned how to collaborate with different stakeholders within my team and at other agencies. What became clear was that everyone saw the same problem through a different lens, and it's easier to get funding (and solve problems) when you take time to gather data and learn about a problem first before you get into solutioning.

To me, becoming a Designer meant combining what I enjoyed the most from my career so far: learning what makes people tick, collaborating with others, and seeing your solutions make an impact in people's lives.

Since changing careers I've graduated with my MS in HCI at DePaul University, done a couple internship and contract stints, and became involved in civic tech through Code for America.

When I'm not designing (and when it's not a pandemic), I do a little comedy. My training background is in clown/physical theater, improv, and storytelling. I've performed awful comedic bits at places like Second City (LA & Chicago), iO West (RIP), and the Annoyance.
